Maintain and clean the grills. Maintain clean burner ports, and periodically check for leaks using a 50/50 mixture of water and dish soap on the hose and its connections. Never leave a grill unattended while it's in use, and keep it away from vinyl siding, fences, and other combustible objects. Baking soda or a fire extinguisher should be used to put out a grease fire. Water will simply make the fire worse.
Keep garbage, mounds of leaves, and firewood out of the house. These items next to your house could easily be used as kindling if a grass fire develops nearby. Keep hot ashes in a metal container and store them far from your house and garage. Never dump them inside or close to your home.
Avoid utilizing plug-in power bars or outlet extenders since they can easily overload a circuit. Never try to fit a three-pronged plug into a two-slot outlet or extension cord; instead, replace any worn-out, broken, or frayed appliance cords.
Regularly have a professional service your heating system, including the chimneys from any wood-burning fireplaces. Keep dust and other combustible objects away from ignition sources and clean up the area around heat sources.
